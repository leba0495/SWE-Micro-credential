Simple Chicken Curry

Ingredients

  • spring onions x 6
  • garlic cloves x 3
  • 200g chopped tomatoes
  • 2tbsp veg oil
  • 2tbsp curry powder
  • 1tsp ground ginger
  • 400g boneless skinless chicken thigh cubed
  • 100ml Greek yoghurt
  • salt and pepper

Recipe

  • Thinly slice most of the spring onions. Peel and chop the garlic.
  • Heat the oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat. Cook the spring onions and garlic for a few minutes.
  • Add the tomatoes, curry powder and ground ginger and cook for 3-4 minutes. Add a splash of water if required.
  • Add the chicken and cook until it is browned on all sides.
  • Add 250ml water, bring to the boil and then reduce to a medium to low heat. Cook for about 15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  • In a separate pan add the rice and then plenty of boiling water. Bring to the boil and then simmer for 10 minutes. Drain the rice and rinse with more boiling water.
  • Take the curry off the heat, stir in the yoghurt then season with salt and pepper.
  • Serve the curry with the rice and a bit more Greek yoghurt.

    • Vegetable Curry

    Ingredients

    • half a medium-sized aubergine (chopped)
    • small carrots (chopped) x 2
    • 100g green beans (chopped in half)
    • One medium-sized potato (chopped)
    • 100g frozen peas
    • 50g desiccated coconut, soaked in a little hot water for a few minutes minutes
    • green chillies (chopped) x 4
    • 1tsp salt
    • medium tomatoes (roughly chopped) x 3
    • 1tbsp plain yoghurt
    • 1tsp garam masala
    • 2tbsp chopped fresh coriander

    Recipe

  • Place the aubergine, carrots, green beans and potato in a medium-sized pan and add 250ml water.
  • Bring to the boil, add the peas, cover and turn the heat to medium. Cook until the vegetables are just tender (about 4 mins).
  • While the veg are cooking, put the coconut, chillies and salt into a food processor. Add 150ml water and blend to form a paste.
  • When the vegetables are just cooked, add the chilli paste. Stir in and simmer gently for around five minutes.
  • Add the tomatoes, yoghurt and garam masala. Gently mix and bring to the boil. One boiling reduce the heat and simmer gently until the potatoes are cooked through (about 3 mins).
  • In a separate pan add the rice and then plenty of boiling water. Bring to the boil and then simmer for 10 minutes. Drain the rice and rinse with more boiling water.
  • Place the vegetable curry into a serving dish and top with the fresh coriander.
  • Serve with the plain rice.

    • Lamb Curry

    Ingredients

    • 1tsp cumin seeds
    • 1tsp coriander seeds
    • Cinnamon stick
    • 1kg leg of lamb cubed
    • onions (finely sliced) x 2
    • garlic cloves (crushed) x 2
    • 2.5cm piece fresh root ginger (finely grated)
    • long red chillies (sliced) x 2
    • 3tbsp tamarind paste
    • 1tsp mango powder
    • 500ml beef stock
    • 90g desiccated coconut
    • 400ml can coconut milk
    • 2tbsp fresh coriander leaves

    Recipe

  • Heat a frying pan until hot, add the cumin and coriander seeds and cinnamon and toast for a few seconds. Place the spices into a pestle and mortar or spice grinder and grind to a powder.
  • Heat a large saucepan pan until hot, add vegetable oil and the lamb. Cook until golden-brown all over. Remove using a slotted spoon and keep for later.
  • Add the onions and cook until soft and golden-brown (about 10 mins).
  • Add the garlic, ginger, chilli, the ground spices and remaining spices. Cook for one minute.
  • Return the lamb to the pan, add the beef stock and bring to a simmer. Cover with a lid and cook over a gentle heat for one hour.
  • Add the dried coconut and the coconut milk and return to a simmer. Cook for another 30 minutes, or until just thickened and the meat is tender. Season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  • In a separate pan add the rice and then plenty of boiling water. Bring to the boil and then simmer for 10 minutes. Drain the rice and rinse with more boiling water.
  • Spoon the lamb curry onto plates with a pile of rice. Serve with a spoonful of yoghurt and some mango chutney.
